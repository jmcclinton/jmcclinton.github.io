Budget-Friendly Summer Meal Ideas



Save your splurging for vacations and still enjoy summer cookouts, get-togethers, and outdoor dining by focusing on budget-friendly meatless options. Using plant-based ingredients helps keep costs down while delivering fresh and clean flavors that won’t heat you up or weigh you down. All you need are the right recipes and a little culinary inspiration.


Unconventional Salads

To make salads more robust, start with a base of grains, noodles, beans, potatoes, or bitter greens. Then, add chopped tomatoes, fresh herbs, diced red onions, dried fruit, roasted sweet potatoes, nuts, or something pickled for extra texture and tang.


Grilled Goodies

A quick sear over a hot flame transforms the flavor of fruits, vegetables, and plant-based proteins. For just a few dollars, you can pick up a head of cabbage, corn, summer squash, asparagus, or any other number of seasonal vegetables and turn them into sides or main dishes. Kebabs with chunks of marinated tofu or tempeh are another fun and inexpensive dinner idea. If you need a quick dessert, try grilled peaches, pineapples, or cantaloupe.


DIY Plant-Based Burger Bar

Supermarket plant-based burgers can get a little pricey, but you can easily make the patties yourself using non-pricey ingredients like legumes, grains, seeds, and mushrooms. Next, lay out your toppings—lettuce, tomatoes, onion, pickled jalapeños, coleslaw, ketchup, etc.—and let everyone make their own custom plant-based burger!


Pizza Party

Pizza may look intimidating, but it’s not difficult to prepare in your oven or, if you want to keep the kitchen cool, on the grill. Making your own dough will save you some bucks, but buying premade dough from the supermarket won’t break the bank. If grilling, just remember to sufficiently oil one side before setting the dough on the grill grates. For toppings, choose items that don’t need a lot of heating or cook time, such as artichoke hearts, olives, sundried tomatoes, or banana peppers. A dessert pizza is also a tasty option if you have extra dough.
